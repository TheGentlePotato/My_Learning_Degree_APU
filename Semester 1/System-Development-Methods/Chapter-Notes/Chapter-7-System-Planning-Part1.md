# Chapter 7 — System Planning Part 1

---

## 1 Introduction to System Planning

System planning is the first stage of the system development process. During this stage organizations identify business problems or opportunities and determine whether an information system should be developed to address them.

The purpose of system planning is to ensure that system development projects support organizational goals and that resources are used effectively. Before starting development, organizations evaluate whether the proposed project is feasible, beneficial, and aligned with business strategy.

System planning helps organizations avoid investing time and resources in projects that are unlikely to succeed.

---

## 2 Objectives of System Planning

System planning helps organizations determine whether a proposed system project should proceed to development.

| Objective | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Identify problems or opportunities | Determine areas where information systems can improve business operations |
| Evaluate project feasibility | Determine whether the project can realistically be completed |
| Estimate costs and benefits | Evaluate whether the system will provide sufficient value |
| Support management decisions | Provide information that helps management approve or reject the project |

System planning ensures that development projects contribute to organizational performance and strategic goals.

---

## 3 Identifying System Projects

Potential system development projects may originate from several sources within an organization.

| Source | Description |
|---|---|
| Organizational strategy | Projects that support long term company goals |
| Management requests | Managers request systems to improve operational efficiency |
| User suggestions | Employees identify problems with current systems |
| Technology opportunities | New technologies create possibilities for new systems |
| External pressures | Regulatory changes or market competition require new systems |

Example

If a company experiences delays in processing customer orders, management may propose an automated order processing system.

---

## 4 System Request

A system request is a formal document that proposes a new information system project.

It summarizes the business problem, the proposed solution, and the expected benefits.

| Component | Description |
|---|---|
| Project sponsor | Person or department requesting the project |
| Business need | Problem or opportunity the system will address |
| Business requirements | Main functions the system should provide |
| Expected business value | Benefits expected from the system |
| Special issues or constraints | Deadlines, regulations, or technical limitations |

Management evaluates the system request before deciding whether the project should move forward.

---

## 5 Feasibility Study

A feasibility study evaluates whether a proposed system project is practical and achievable.

The purpose of feasibility analysis is to determine whether the organization should proceed with system development.

Feasibility studies help reduce risk and ensure that resources are invested in valuable projects.

---

## 6 Types of Feasibility

Several types of feasibility must be evaluated when analyzing a system proposal.

| Type of Feasibility | Description |
|---|---|
| Technical feasibility | Determines whether required technology and expertise are available |
| Economic feasibility | Determines whether the financial benefits justify the development costs |
| Operational feasibility | Determines whether the system will function effectively within the organization |
| Schedule feasibility | Determines whether the project can be completed within the required time frame |
| Organizational feasibility | Determines whether the system aligns with organizational goals and culture |

These evaluations help management decide whether the project should proceed.

---

## 7 Technical Feasibility

Technical feasibility evaluates whether the organization has the necessary technology and technical expertise to develop the proposed system.

Important considerations include:

• Availability of suitable hardware and software  
• Skills and experience of the development team  
• Compatibility with existing systems  
• System performance requirements

Example

If a company wants to develop an artificial intelligence system but lacks AI expertise, technical feasibility may be low.

---

## 8 Economic Feasibility

Economic feasibility evaluates whether the financial benefits of the proposed system exceed its development and operational costs.

Organizations perform cost benefit analysis to determine whether the project is financially worthwhile.

Costs may include:

• Hardware and software acquisition  
• System development costs  
• Maintenance and support costs  
• Training costs

Benefits may include increased revenue, cost reduction, and improved productivity.

---

## 9 Tangible and Intangible Benefits

Benefits from information systems may be classified as tangible or intangible.

| Benefit Type | Description | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Tangible benefits | Benefits that can be measured financially | Increased revenue, reduced operational costs |
| Intangible benefits | Benefits that are difficult to measure financially | Improved customer satisfaction, better decision making |

Although intangible benefits are difficult to quantify, they are often important for long term success.

---

## 10 Operational Feasibility

Operational feasibility evaluates whether the proposed system will operate successfully within the organization.

Important considerations include:

• User acceptance  
• Organizational workflows  
• Training requirements  
• System usability

If users resist using the system, the project may fail even if the technology functions correctly.

---

## 11 Schedule Feasibility

Schedule feasibility evaluates whether the project can be completed within the required timeframe.

Factors influencing schedule feasibility include:

• Project complexity  
• Availability of development staff  
• Project deadlines  
• Potential risks and delays

Example

If management requires the system within two months but development requires six months, the project is not schedule feasible.

---

## 12 Cost Benefit Analysis

Cost benefit analysis compares the expected costs of system development with the expected benefits.

| Category | Examples |
|---|---|
| Development costs | Programmer salaries, development tools |
| Operational costs | Maintenance, system upgrades |
| Tangible benefits | Increased revenue, reduced operating costs |
| Intangible benefits | Improved customer satisfaction, improved decision making |

Organizations use this analysis to determine whether the investment in the system is worthwhile.

---

## 13 Project Selection

Organizations often have multiple potential projects but limited resources. Therefore management must prioritize which projects should be developed.

Project selection involves evaluating projects based on feasibility, expected benefits, and strategic importance.

| Selection Method | Description |
|---|---|
| Value based selection | Projects selected based on expected business value |
| Feasibility based selection | Projects selected based on feasibility results |
| Strategic alignment | Projects selected based on alignment with business strategy |

Projects that provide the highest organizational value are usually selected.

---

## 14 Project Planning Activities

Once a project is approved, developers begin planning the development process.

Project planning includes:

• Defining project scope  
• Identifying required resources  
• Estimating development time  
• Organizing development tasks  
• Identifying possible risks

Proper planning helps ensure that the project proceeds efficiently and meets its objectives.

---

## 15 Project Scheduling

Project scheduling organizes development tasks into a timeline to ensure that activities are completed in the correct order.

Scheduling allows project managers to monitor progress and manage resources effectively.

Common scheduling tools include:

| Tool | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Gantt chart | Displays project tasks and their duration |
| Project timeline | Visual representation of the project schedule |
| Task dependencies | Shows relationships between development tasks |

Scheduling helps ensure that the project is completed within the required time frame.

---

## Chapter Summary

System planning is the first stage of system development and focuses on identifying business problems, evaluating proposed system projects, and determining whether development should proceed. During this stage organizations analyze system requests, perform feasibility studies, and conduct cost benefit analysis to evaluate the practicality and value of the proposed system. Feasibility analysis examines technical, economic, operational, schedule, and organizational factors to determine whether the project is achievable. Organizations then select projects that align with strategic goals and provide the greatest business value. Effective system planning and careful project management ensure that development resources are used efficiently and increase the likelihood of successful system implementation.
