# Chapter 8 — System Planning Part 2

---

## 1 Introduction to System Planning Part 2

System Planning Part 2 focuses on understanding the system requirements and identifying possible risks before system development begins. Once a project has been approved during the earlier planning stage, the development team must clearly understand what the system should do and what problems might occur during development.

Two important concepts are introduced in this stage: Requirement Engineering and Project Risk Management. Requirement engineering focuses on identifying and documenting the needs of the system, while risk management focuses on identifying and controlling potential problems that may affect the success of the project.

These activities are essential because unclear requirements and unmanaged risks are two of the most common causes of project failure in system development.

---

## 2 Requirement Engineering

Requirement Engineering is the process of identifying, gathering, analyzing, refining, and documenting the requirements of a system.

The purpose of requirement engineering is to ensure that developers clearly understand the needs of the users and the organization before designing and building the system.

Requirement engineering acts as a communication bridge between users, analysts, and developers. It ensures that the system being developed actually solves the business problem that initiated the project.

Without proper requirement engineering, developers may build systems that do not meet user expectations or fail to solve the original problem.

---

## 3 Objectives of Requirement Engineering

Requirement engineering is performed to achieve several objectives.

| Objective | Explanation |
|---|---|
| Understand business problems | Identify the problem that the system must solve |
| Identify system requirements | Determine what features the system must include |
| Improve communication | Ensure users and developers have the same understanding |
| Reduce development errors | Clear requirements reduce misunderstandings |
| Support system design | Requirements provide the foundation for system design |

Clear requirements help prevent costly redesign during later development stages.

---

## 4 Types of System Requirements

System requirements are usually classified into different categories.

| Requirement Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Functional requirements | Describe what the system should do |
| Non functional requirements | Describe system quality and performance |
| High level requirements | General description of system goals |
| Low level requirements | Detailed technical description of system functions |

Functional requirements focus on system features.

Example  
The system should allow users to register for courses online.

Non functional requirements describe system performance.

Example  
The system should respond to user requests within three seconds.

---

## 5 High Level and Low Level Requirements

Requirements are often first defined at a high level and later refined into detailed specifications.

| Requirement Level | Explanation |
|---|---|
| High level requirement | Broad description of system objectives |
| Low level requirement | Detailed explanation of system functions |

Example

High level requirement  
The system should support student registration.

Low level requirement  
The system should allow students to select courses, verify prerequisites, and confirm registration.

Low level requirements provide the detailed information required for system design.

---

## 6 Investigative Questions in Requirement Analysis

System analysts often use a set of investigative questions to understand how the current system works and how it can be improved.

These questions include:

Who  
What  
Where  
When  
How  
Why

| Question | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Who | Identify the people involved in the system |
| What | Identify tasks performed by users |
| Where | Identify where operations occur |
| When | Identify timing of activities |
| How | Identify procedures used to perform tasks |
| Why | Identify the reason the activity exists |

The question "why" is particularly important because it allows analysts to determine whether an activity is necessary or whether it can be improved or eliminated.

---

## 7 Requirement Engineering Process

Requirement engineering is usually performed through several stages.

| Stage | Description |
|---|---|
| Requirement elicitation | Gathering requirements from stakeholders |
| Requirement analysis | Evaluating and organizing requirements |
| Requirement documentation | Recording requirements in a structured form |
| Requirement validation | Checking requirements for accuracy and completeness |
| Requirement management | Monitoring requirement changes during development |

This structured process ensures that system requirements are clear, consistent, and well documented.

---

## 8 Requirement Elicitation

Requirement elicitation is the process of collecting information from stakeholders to understand the current system and identify new system requirements.

Stakeholders may include users, managers, customers, and technical staff.

Sources of information include:

Users and employees  
Managers and decision makers  
Existing system documentation  
Business process descriptions

The goal is to discover user needs and system expectations.

---

## 9 Requirement Elicitation Techniques

Several techniques are commonly used to gather system requirements.

| Technique | Description |
|---|---|
| Interviews | Direct discussions with users or stakeholders |
| Questionnaires | Surveys used to collect information from many users |
| Observation | Analysts observe how users perform tasks |
| Document analysis | Reviewing existing system documentation |
| Workshops | Group meetings involving multiple stakeholders |

Using multiple techniques improves the accuracy and completeness of requirements.

---

## 10 Requirement Documentation

After requirements are collected and analyzed, they are documented in a structured format.

The most common document used to record system requirements is the System Requirements Specification (SRS).

The SRS document typically includes:

System overview  
Functional requirements  
Non functional requirements  
System constraints  
System assumptions

The SRS serves as the foundation for system design and development.

---

## 11 Requirement Validation

Requirement validation ensures that the documented requirements accurately represent user needs.

Validation checks whether requirements are:

Correct  
Complete  
Consistent  
Feasible

Validation activities may include:

Requirement reviews  
User confirmation  
Prototype evaluation

This step prevents costly development errors.

---

## 12 Requirement Management

During system development, requirements may change due to new business needs or technological changes.

Requirement management tracks and controls these changes.

Requirement management activities include:

Tracking requirement changes  
Updating requirement documents  
Ensuring all changes are approved

Effective requirement management helps maintain project stability.

---

## 13 Project Risk Management

Project risk management involves identifying potential risks that may affect the success of the system development project.

A risk is an uncertain event that may negatively affect project objectives such as cost, schedule, or system performance.

Risk management helps project managers prepare strategies to prevent or reduce these problems.

---

## 14 Types of Project Risks

Several types of risks may occur during system development.

| Risk Type | Description |
|---|---|
| Technical risk | New technology may fail or perform poorly |
| Schedule risk | Development may take longer than expected |
| Cost risk | Project may exceed the budget |
| Requirement risk | Requirements may change frequently |
| Organizational risk | Management changes or organizational restructuring |

Understanding these risks allows project managers to prepare appropriate responses.

---

## 15 Risk Management Process

Risk management usually involves several steps.

| Step | Description |
|---|---|
| Risk identification | Identify possible risks |
| Risk analysis | Evaluate likelihood and impact |
| Risk mitigation | Develop strategies to reduce risk |
| Risk monitoring | Continuously track risks during development |

Risk management improves project planning and reduces uncertainty.

---

## 16 Importance of Requirement Engineering and Risk Management

Requirement engineering and risk management are essential for successful system development.

Requirement engineering ensures that developers build the correct system, while risk management ensures that potential problems are controlled.

When both activities are performed properly, the project team can develop systems more efficiently and reduce the likelihood of project failure.

---

## Chapter Summary

System Planning Part 2 focuses on requirement engineering and project risk management. Requirement engineering helps analysts identify and document the needs of users and the organization before system development begins. It involves gathering requirements through techniques such as interviews, questionnaires, observation, and document analysis, and then organizing and validating these requirements to ensure they are accurate and complete. Requirements may include functional requirements that describe system features and non functional requirements that describe system performance and quality. In addition, project risk management helps identify potential technical, financial, and organizational risks that may affect the project. By identifying, analyzing, and monitoring risks early in the development process, organizations can improve project planning and increase the chances of successful system implementation.
